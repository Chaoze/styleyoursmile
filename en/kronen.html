Dental crowns

Ceramic restorations from our in-house CAD/CAM lab - natural, stable and long-lasting.

What is a dental crown?

A dental crown is a custom-made protective cover that surrounds and stabilises a damaged or weakened tooth. It restores shape, strength and appearance and helps preserve the natural tooth for many years.

Unlike a filling, which repairs only part of the tooth, a crown covers the entire visible part above the gum line. The result is a durable restoration with a natural look and feel.

When is a crown recommended?

A crown is often the right solution when a filling can no longer offer enough stability. Typical reasons include:

  • After root canal treatment, when the tooth needs extra protection
  • Severe decay or wear affecting a large part of the tooth
  • Fractures or cracks that cannot be restored with a simple filling
  • Aesthetic corrections for discoloured or misshapen teeth

Materials at a glance

Depending on your clinical situation and aesthetic goals, different crown materials may be suitable.

Full-cast crowns

Made from gold alloy or another metal. Very durable and especially suitable for back teeth with high chewing forces.

Porcelain-fused-to-metal crowns

A strong metal framework covered with ceramic. A proven option that combines stability with a natural appearance.

All-ceramic crowns

Made entirely of high-quality zirconia or lithium disilicate. Highly aesthetic, metal-free and ideal for a natural result.

Galvano crowns

A thin electroformed gold coping combined with ceramic. Extremely precise and very biocompatible.

CEREC - crowns in a single visit

With our CEREC system, we can scan, design and mill many ceramic crowns directly in the practice - often in a single appointment. That means no temporary and no second visit for many cases.

How CEREC works

  1. Digital 3D scan: We capture your tooth precisely with an intraoral scanner, without conventional impressions.
  2. Digital design: Your crown is designed on screen with high precision.
  3. In-practice milling: The restoration is milled from a ceramic block in our practice.
  4. Final bonding: The crown is tried in, adjusted and permanently bonded.

For more complex cases or layered aesthetic work, we also collaborate with our in-house lab. In those cases, production usually takes around one week.
