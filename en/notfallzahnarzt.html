Acute tooth pain? We help quickly and directly.

Immediate measures in a dental emergency

  1. 1Stop bleeding: Press a clean cloth or gauze pad firmly against the affected area and bite down gently to help stop the bleeding.
  2. 2Store a knocked-out tooth correctly: Never let the tooth dry out and do not scrub or clean the root. Place it in fresh milk or, if no milk is available, keep it carefully in the cheek. Time is critical.
  3. 3Contact a dentist immediately: Call us without delay. The faster you reach us, the better the chance of saving the tooth.

Immediate help in dental emergencies

Tooth pain rarely happens at a convenient moment. Whether it is a broken tooth, a knocked-out tooth, an acute infection or severe pulpal pain, we reserve daily capacity for emergencies and offer short-notice appointments whenever possible.

Acute dental pain should always be taken seriously. Pain starting in the jaw can radiate to the neck, temple, ear or shoulder and can be difficult to localise. A fast dental assessment is therefore essential to identify the cause and begin the right treatment.

At your emergency appointment, we first carry out a focused pain assessment with x-rays, sensitivity tests and a clinical examination. Based on this, we initiate the necessary measures immediately, from pain relief and temporary stabilisation to definitive treatment.

Transparent communication

Even in an emergency, we take the time to explain what has happened and which treatment options are available. We show you the x-rays, discuss the advantages and disadvantages of different approaches and help you make an informed decision.

If more than one treatment path is possible, we explain all alternatives openly, from short-term pain relief to longer-term solutions such as an implant, crown or root canal treatment.

Insurance

If the dental injury is accident-related, for example after a fall, sports accident or traffic accident, the costs may under certain circumstances be covered by accident insurance or supplementary health insurance.

We recommend informing your insurer as soon as possible after the accident. Our practice can provide the required documentation, including reports, x-rays and invoices.
