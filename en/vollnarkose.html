General anaesthesia for dental treatment

A safe option for extensive procedures, pronounced anxiety or special treatment situations.

What does treatment under general anaesthesia mean?

General anaesthesia puts the patient into a controlled sleep state so that dental treatment can be carried out without conscious awareness. It is organised together with experienced anaesthesia specialists and considered when the expected benefit clearly outweighs simpler options.

When can it be useful?

  • For extensive treatment that would otherwise require several very stressful appointments
  • For severe dental anxiety when other methods are not sufficient
  • For a pronounced gag reflex or special medical and practical situations
  • For patients who can only be treated with great difficulty in a conventional setting

The usual process

  1. Consultation: we discuss your treatment needs, medical history and whether general anaesthesia is a sensible choice.
  2. Pre-assessment: depending on the case, additional checks are organised to ensure the anaesthesia can be performed safely.
  3. Treatment day: the planned dental procedure is carried out efficiently in one session together with the anaesthesia team.
  4. Recovery and aftercare: afterwards you are monitored and receive clear instructions for the period after treatment.

Important to know

General anaesthesia is not the first step for every patient. In many situations, local anaesthetic, hypnosis or other calming approaches may already be sufficient. We advise you carefully so that the chosen solution fits both the treatment and your comfort level.

Discuss treatment in a calm setting

If you are wondering whether general anaesthesia could be appropriate in your case, we will be happy to review the options with you personally.
