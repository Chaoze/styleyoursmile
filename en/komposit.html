Composite fillings

High-quality tooth-coloured restorations - the modern alternative to amalgam.

What is composite?

Composite is a tooth-coloured filling material made of fine ceramic particles and a light-cured resin matrix. It combines the adaptability of resin with the improved strength and wear resistance of ceramic-reinforced material.

Modern composite fillings can be matched closely to the natural tooth colour and provide a discreet and reliable restoration for many situations.

Why composite instead of amalgam?

Composite is metal-free and mercury-free and can be colour-matched very accurately. Existing amalgam fillings can be safely replaced in our practice with aesthetic composite restorations.

Advantages

  • Matched to the natural tooth colour
  • Mercury-free and metal-free
  • Natural-looking aesthetics
  • Reliable durability with good care
  • Conservative bonding technique that can preserve tooth structure

Typical applications

Composite is suitable not only for caries treatment, but also for small repairs after fractures, replacing old fillings, closing minor gaps or improving the shape of a tooth in a minimally invasive way.

How treatment works

After removing decay or defective filling material, the tooth is prepared and isolated. The composite is placed in layers, cured with a special light, shaped precisely and polished for a natural finish.
