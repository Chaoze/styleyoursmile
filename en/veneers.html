Veneers in Zurich

Ultra-thin ceramic veneers for natural, long-lasting and minimally invasive smile aesthetics.

What are veneers?

Veneers are extremely thin, light-transmitting ceramic shells bonded to the visible front surfaces of the teeth. These shells, often only 0.3 to 0.5 millimetres thick, mimic the natural translucency of enamel and create a highly natural-looking result.

Compared with crowns, veneers offer an important advantage: considerably less healthy tooth structure has to be removed. While a crown covers the entire tooth, veneers usually require only minimal preparation of the surface.

At our practice on Weinbergstrasse 62 in Zurich, we work with an in-house CAD/CAM lab and an experienced master dental technician. Each veneer is individually designed for you, for a result that looks and feels like a natural tooth.

Your advantages

  • Natural appearance through individually planned shape, size and shade
  • Long-lasting results, often 10 to 15 years with good care
  • Minimally invasive - much less tooth reduction than with crowns
  • Usually completed in only two main appointments
  • Suitable for a range of aesthetic concerns

How treatment works

Our veneer process focuses on precision and comfort. In most cases, two main appointments are needed, from the first consultation to final bonding.

  1. Initial consultation - We discuss your wishes, choose the ideal shade and take a digital 3D scan of your teeth. Based on this, we create a mock-up so you can preview your future smile.
  2. Mock-up fitting with our master technician - The provisional mock-up is tried directly in your mouth. Together we refine shape, length and colour until the design feels right.
  3. Tooth preparation - A very thin layer of enamel is gently refined, a digital impression is taken and your teeth are protected with high-quality temporaries.
  4. Veneer try-in - The finished ceramic veneers are placed for fitting and final adjustments. Only once you are fully satisfied do we proceed to the final step.
  5. Final bonding - The veneers are permanently bonded with a high-quality light-cured adhesive. Your treatment is complete and your new smile is ready.

When are veneers suitable?

Veneers are an excellent solution for many aesthetic concerns. During the consultation, we assess together whether veneers are the best option for you. Typical indications include:

  • Discoloured teeth that do not respond well to whitening
  • Chipped or worn teeth, for example due to grinding
  • Gaps that are aesthetically disturbing
  • Mild misalignment or irregular tooth shape
  • General dissatisfaction with the appearance of the smile

Care after treatment

With proper care, veneers often last 10 to 15 years or longer. The following measures help you protect your new smile:

  • Brushing: Use a soft toothbrush and a non-abrasive fluoride toothpaste. Brush gently and thoroughly at least twice a day.
  • Staining foods and drinks: Coffee, red wine, tea and smoking can affect the surface over time. Enjoy them in moderation and rinse with water afterwards.
  • Night guard: If you grind your teeth, we strongly recommend a custom guard. It protects both your veneers and your natural tooth structure.
  • Professional cleanings: Regular hygiene visits and check-ups, typically every 6 months, help us keep your veneers in excellent condition.
  • Avoid hard biting: Veneers should not be used to bite hard objects such as nuts, ice or packaging.
